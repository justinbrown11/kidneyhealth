Sodium

    Foods low in sodium

      Seasoning salts

      Sauces (i.e. barbecue, teryaki, soy)

      Cured meats (i.e. ham, bacon)

      Lunch Meats

      Salted Snacks (i.e. chips, pretzels, popcorn)

    Foods high in sodium

      Oranges, bananas

      Potatoes, tomatoes

      Brown and wild rice

      Dairy foods

      Whole wheat breads and pasta

Reducing Protein

    Eat small portions of protein-rich foods. Recommended amounts are as follows

      Meat / poultry: 2-3 oz

      Dairy: ½ cup milk/yogurt, 1 slice cheese

      Beans: ½ cup cooked

      Nuts: ¼ cup

      Grains: ½ cup cooked

Phosphorus

    Foods low in phosphorus

      Fresh fruits and veggies

      Breads, pastas, rice

      Corn or rice cereals

      Light-colored sodas

    Foods high in phosphorus

      Meat, poultry, fish

      Bran and oatmeal

      Dairy foods

      Beans, lentils, nuts

      Dark-colored sodas

Potassium

    Foods low in potassium

      Apples, peaches

      Carrots, green beans

      White bread and pasta

      White rice

      Cooked rice, wheat cereals

      Apple, grape, cranberry juice

    Foods high in potassium

      Oranges, bananas

      Potatoes, tomatoes

      Brown and wild rice

      Dairy foods

      Whole wheat breads and pasta
