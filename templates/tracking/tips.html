    Eating Out Guide

Reducing Protein

    Eat small portions of protein-rich foods. Recommended amounts are as follows:

      Meat / poultry: 2-3 oz

      Dairy: ½ cup milk/yogurt, 1 slice cheese

      Beans: ½ cup cooked

      Nuts: ¼ cup

      Grains: ½ cup cooked

Sodium

    Foods high in sodium

      Seasoning salts

      Sauces (i.e. barbecue, teriyaki, soy)

      Cured meats (i.e. ham, bacon)

      Lunch Meats

      Salted Snacks (i.e. chips, pretzels, popcorn)

    Foods low in sodium

      Fresh herbs

      Fresh meats

      Fresh vegtables

      Canned and fresh fruits

      Homemade meals

Phosphorus

    Foods low in phosphorus

      Fresh fruits and veggies

      Breads, pastas, rice

      Rice milk (not enriched)

      Corn or rice cereals

      Light-colored sodas

    Foods high in phosphorus

      Meat, poultry, fish

      Bran and oatmeal

      Dairy foods

      Beans, lentils, nuts

      Dark-colored sodas, fruit punch

Potassium

    Foods low in potassium

      Apples, peaches

      Carrots, green beans

      White bread and pasta

      White rice

      Rice milk (not enriched)

      Cooked rice, wheat cereals

      Apple, grape, cranberry juice

    Foods high in potassium

      Oranges, bananas, orange juice

      Potatoes, tomatoes

      Brown and wild rice

      Bran cereals

      Dairy products

      Whole wheat breads and pasta

      Beans and Nuts

These suggestions are given by the National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases
